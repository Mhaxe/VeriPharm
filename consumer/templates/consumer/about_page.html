About Veripharm

The proliferation of counterfeit medicines in Ghana presents a critical public health concern that can no longer be overlooked. Across sub-Saharan Africa, it is estimated that counterfeit drugs contribute to over 500,000 deaths annually, with approximately 267,000 deaths linked to substandard or falsified antimalarial medications and 169,000 deaths resulting from ineffective or fake antibiotics used to treat severe paediatric pneumonia (WHO, 2023).

The pharmaceutical supply chain—an intricate network spanning various organizations and geographical regions—plays a central role in ensuring safe medicine distribution. However, its inherent complexity often introduces vulnerabilities, such as fragmented data sources, inaccurate records, and insufficient transparency. These gaps create opportunities for the infiltration of counterfeit drugs, posing severe health risks and leading to significant financial losses within the healthcare sector.

As a result, there is a growing demand for a secure, end-to-end drug traceability system that can provide full visibility across the pharmaceutical supply chain. Traditional centralized tracking systems have proven inadequate, frequently falling short in key areas such as data authenticity, privacy protection, transparency, scalability, and system resilience. These shortcomings are regularly exploited by counterfeiters, undermining public trust and drug safety.

To address these challenges, this project proposes a blockchain-based pharmaceutical traceability system built on the Ethereum platform. By leveraging smart contracts, the system records and verifies drug transactions at every stage of the supply chain. These smart contracts ensure immutable, tamper-proof records and establish a trusted source of data provenance among all stakeholders involved.

Preliminary implementation results demonstrate promising outcomes: a notable reduction in counterfeit drug circulation, enhanced consumer confidence in pharmaceutical products, and increased transparency and accountability among supply chain actors. By discouraging fraudulent activities and guaranteeing traceable transactions, the system strengthens the integrity of Ghana's pharmaceutical supply chain and reinforces public health safeguards.

Modus operandum

The main objective of this study is to design and implement a blockchain-based drug traceability system for Ghana's pharmaceutical supply chain, incorporating measures:

  • Blockchain-based Traceability Platform
  • QR-Code Integration for Real-Time Tracking
  • AI-Powered Supply-Chain Monitoring
  • Stakeholder-Centric User Interfaces

Significance of the Study

This project addresses a major gap in pharmaceutical regulation in Ghana by providing a verifiable method to confirm drug authenticity and prevent unauthorized use. It aligns with Ghana's SDG priorities, particularly SDG 3, SDG 9, and SDG 16, by enhancing healthcare safety, promoting technological infrastructure, and strengthening regulatory institutions. If effectively implemented, the system can serve as a replicable model for other countries facing similar pharmaceutical challenges.
